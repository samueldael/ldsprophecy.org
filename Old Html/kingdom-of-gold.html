Christ's Kingdom of Gold was the Kingdom of Heaven

Most every religion attempts to interpret Nebuchadnezzar’s dream of the great image. The reason is quite simple. The Lord intended to record something for our time that man will not destroy. If the truth came clearly, no one of that time could even understand and when it came time for the prophecy to come to its full, the evil intentions of men would change it or delete it so they would not have to answer to the truth. The Lord has to be careful not to be too clear. The religious mind cannot see this. We prefer all prophecy to pertain to the past or the future. Because of the allegory, prophesy easily fits history repeatedly so each generation can get something out of it. Fundamentally, The Lord reserves all prophecy for a time when God cannot speak because of sinful acts. Fortunately, there are so many prophecies telling of the same thing in different allegories that surly the truth will come about even in an interval of chaos and troubling times. This is how the power of God is manifest in the final hours.

Head of Gold: If Jesus Christ is the King of Kings and the head of gold, Daniel is telling us what will be during the existence of Christianity and how all things will end. This article covers the kingdom of gold in its beginning and how it relates to all of the other kingdoms.

The Great Tree: “Then Daniel, whose name was Belteshazzar, was astonied for one hour, and his thoughts troubled him. The king spake, and said, Belteshazzar, let not the dream, or the interpretation thereof, trouble thee. Belteshazzar answered and said, My lord, the dream be to them that hate thee, and the interpretation thereof to thine enemies” (Daniel 4:19). Here Daniel is astonished for one hour in trying to understand the second dream. Then Daniel suggested the dream was to those who hate the king. “The tree that thou sawest, which grew, and was strong, whose height reached unto the heaven, and the sight thereof to all the earth;” (Daniel 4:20). If the kingdom reaches heaven, this is the allegory of a people living the order of Enoch as at first. “Whose leaves were fair, and the fruit thereof much, and in it was meat for all; under which the beasts of the field dwelt, and upon whose branches the fowls of the heaven had their habitation:” (Daniel 4:21-22). Meat for all is the gospel of Jesus for all the earth with fruit to spare. The beasts of the field or many other kingdoms will seek habitation under this tree. Daniel compares the tree to king Nebuchadnezzar to cover in allegory the coming of Jesus Christ and his gospel of heaven.

The Four Beasts: “Daniel spake and said, I saw in my vision by night, and, behold, the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea. And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another. The first was like a lion, and had eagle’s wings” (Daniel 7:2â€“4). The four beasts correspond to the four metals in the dream of the great image. The first is like a lion. Here the kingdom of heaven restored by John is in likeness to the king of beasts.

The Four Kings: “ And now will I shew thee the truth. Behold, there shall stand up yet three kings in Persia; and the fourth shall be far richer than they all: and by his strength through his riches he shall stir up all against the realm of Grecia. And a mighty king shall stand up, that shall rule with great dominion, and do according to his will” (Daniel 11:2-3). Like the great image of four metals, the four kings exemplify the four kingdoms. The mighty king is Christ when his kingdom of heaven stands up. This heaven will rule with great dominion and do according to the will of the Father.

This is only four of Daniel’s dreams and visions that foretell the Kingdom of Heaven announced by John the Baptist. These four carry similarities that demonstrate four witnesses from different visions carrying key words such as gold, lion, reaching heaven, and a kingdom according to His will. As to heaven and the new wine Christ introduced, “For how great is his goodness, and how great is his beauty! corn shall make the young men cheerful, and new wine the maids” (Zechariah 9:17). There are many prophecies that foretell Christ. They either speak of his birth, the coming of heaven or something that describes the rejection of Christ.

When John announced the “Kingdom of Heaven is at hand” this was the first of four kingdoms of Christianity. When you apply all of the visions of Daniel to the span of Christianity then the command to bring about everlasting righteous before the seventy weeks ends, makes more sense than secular kingdoms since Daniel. When those who realized the implications of the seventy-week prophecy then all of the visions of Daniel should match with the history of Christianity. We can then learn the considered the more important parts of history as well as determine the most destructive. By understanding past prophecy we can then look to the solutions the Lord expects of us.
