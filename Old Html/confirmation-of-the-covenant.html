The Confirmation of the Covenant

The confirmation of the covenant comes from Daniel. “And he shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and in the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease, and for the overspreading of abominations he shall make it desolate, even until the consummation, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate” (Daniel 9:27). Such covenant is very limited in time and suffered a decline by the abomination or the Kingdom of Iron. The prophecies of Daniel delivered a new nation for Heaven. At no other time in history since Jacob other than at the time of Christ when the Roman Empire provided freedom of religion, could heaven come again. It lasted a short time, but the Lord knew that it would.

Four Beasts: goes directly to the Kingdom of Iron.

Ram and He Goat: “And out of one of them came forth a little horn, which waxed exceeding great, toward the south, and toward the east, and toward the pleasant land. And it waxed great, even to the host of heaven; and it cast down some of the host and of the stars to the ground, and stamped upon them” (Daniel 8:9–10). This is after the notable horn when four came up after indicating the Reformation. Then came up a little horn from the four, which waxed exceeding great towards the south meaning traditional Christianity,  towards the east meaning new wisdom and even to the host of heaven, meaning heaven was given to the little horn (Joseph Smith). Before Daniel could say anything else, this restored heaven fell to the ground. It also brought down the stars and the saints stamped upon this restored heaven shortly after its birth. This was the beginning of the Kingdom of Iron. It was also the beginning of the 1260 days and seven half times until the final redemption

Four Kings: “Then shall stand up in his estate a raiser of taxes in the glory of the kingdom: but within few days he shall be destroyed, neither in anger, nor in battle” (Daniel 11:20). This exemplifies Joseph Smith that delivered the Abrahamic tithe by revelation. Within a few days, he suffered death. It was not by anger of the Lord, not under battle. His death sealed the testimony of heaven, which remains clothed in sackcloth until the 1260 days ends with the final redemption. This comes from Revelation.

The bible and Book of Mormon are very limited on the restoration of this covenant. Daniel is explicit, but even the Mormons do not use this reference because they do not look upon themselves as the holy people that destroyed the sanctuary even though prophecy from Daniel, Isaiah, Revelations, and many more prophets express a fall and a redemption. Mormons attribute the redemption prophecies as the restoration and neglect the concept that the holy people can only destroy something that the Lord restores. As Christ said that this generation (meaning the generation of those stars that fall from heaven and the moon turn to blood) will not pass until all is fulfilled. The saints cannot fall unless they first receive. “Let no man deceive you by any means: for that day shall not come, except there come a falling away first, and that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition” (2 Thessalonians 2:3). Mormons attribute this falling away of the first church and not the fall of the Mormons. The difference between the first church and the Mormons is a distance of time. With the Mormons, everything happens in one generation. With the first church, it took many generations to lose the true meaning of heaven.

One has to read the Doctrine and Covenants in order to get a grander vision of the confirmation of the covenant predicted by Daniel. In this record, visions as described in the mount transfiguration happened to Joseph Smith and others. Even the Book of Mormon came forth with many witnesses who saw the brass plates and three saw the angel Moroni in addition to Joseph Smith. Why would the Lord give so much just to have it fall in thirty years? If one can see the power of God, they will realize that when prophecy finely reveals the history of Christianity and the abomination of the Mormon Church, they will realize that Joseph Smith received it all otherwise, the saints could not fall. Understanding this will prepare a remnant of the Mormons along with many Jews that will see how the visions of Daniel and other prophets compare with Revelations and come to see that their fathers rejected Christ. The whole purpose of the confirmation of the covenant followed by a fall is to match it with prophecy. Then we can build Zion without hand—something the Lord always knew would be the only way. Whenever the Lord bares his arm, what he gives always fails. If we come to understand then it is we who build according to the written prophecies and not according to the hand of prophet, priest, or king.
