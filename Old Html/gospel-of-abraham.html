The Gospel of Abraham

When Christ ascended about forty days after his resurrection, He said unto his disciples, “It is not for you to know the times or the seasons, which the Father hath put in his own power. But ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judæa, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth. And when he had spoken these things, while they beheld, he was taken up; and a cloud received him out of their sight.” What we perhaps deem as angles stood by saying, “Ye men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up…? this same Jesus, which is taken up from you…., shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go…”.

I specifically leave out references to heaven because of the literal interpretation of “he was taken up”. If a cloud received him out of their sight, what does up mean? The writer assumes like most of us that heaven is always up relative to where we are standing. The universe is circular and up is relative. It more likely means the rising to a higher state, which can be anywhere.

Now, how did Christ come and go in the first place? Does Christianity “stand gazing” at his physical birth, his physical baptism, his physical death and even his physical resurrection and neglect the meaning and purpose of his ultimate mission? Is this what the angels tried to imply? We tend to look at the physical aspects and neglect the ultimate meaning of things. I use this example for two reasons. First, from the beginning of Christianity we were not to know the times and seasons until the time of the end. Second, these type of visions happen when the Kingdom of Heaven is established. Destroy the principles of heaven and we destroy revelation.

The Lord established the economic law of heaven through Joseph Smith for a purpose. It fulfilled prophecy pertaining to the confirmation of the covenant in the last days. When you understand Daniel, you will understand this. Joseph had many visions restoring many things that had been lost since the original church over time rejected the law of heaven. Although Joseph Smith received revelations and several visions, all ended because the Mormons also rejected this law of heaven and thus lost the revelation needed to sustain it. This article tries to explain why.

The last vision of Joseph Smith was spectacular. It expressed a similar event as the Mount of Transfiguration in the New Testament. The event took place in the newly dedicated Kirtland Temple. Not only did those present see Christ, but also three visitor came to restore certain keys. They were Moses, Elias, and Elijah. It is not the intent to explain what keys mean other than the obtaining of knowledge. The vision is as follows:

“The veil was taken from our minds, and the eyes of our understanding were opened.
