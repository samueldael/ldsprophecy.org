The Redemption of Zion

As in a previous article, knowledge of the times and seasons mentioned by the Lord would not be had among the apostles. Mormons would certainly agree that the fall of Christianity through the dark ages would most certainly cover any attempt to understand the times and the seasons. The question remains if current members or prophets do understand the times and the seasons. Did Joseph Smith receive such knowledge? Daniel’s interpretation of the great image gives a secular result of the future and every religious interpretation from the Jew to the protestants at the time of the restoration offer a similar knowledge as to the times and season of this dream. Joseph Fielding Smith duplicated the same secular tenancy to consider King Nebuchadnezzar the start of all the times and seasons of this prophecy. In general, he followed from Nebuchadnezzar through the Medes and the Persians eventually to Rome as the kingdom of Brass that would rule the entire world. This eventually ended in ten toes of the great image, which he said represented the then European Kingdoms, which evolved, from Rome/Byzantium or western civilization. In the days of these kings (ten toes) shall the God of Heaven set up a kingdom that shall never be destroyed. Smith concluded in the presents of a body of missionaries that the Church, founded in 1830 at a time when all ten kings ruled Europe, defined this kingdom. Joseph Fielding Smith demonstrated scriptorium abilities, but in this case, a missionary asked, “How do you know this?” Smith answered; “Because I said so.” Is this the arrogance that comes from not living the Laws of Heaven and the redemption requires? The interpretation did not demonstrate the times and the seasons of prophecy. Smith also said that no one would walk on the moon. Arrogance never sees the truth.

Most of these secular interpretations come from Protestant origins and even as far back as Jewish theory.  Joseph Smith assumed the Restored Church was the stone cut out of the mountain without hands. It thus would fill the earth and not be left to other people. He even compared “the Gospel in the days of Messiah's earthly ministry, the church, sometimes called the kingdom of God, and the kingdom of heaven?  Was it not "left to other people?"  Messiah himself said of the Jews, "Therefore say I unto you, the kingdom of God shall be taken from you and given to a nation bringing forth the fruits thereof”” (History of the Church, Vol 1, Introduction, p.xxxix). Although Joseph fallowed the general secular interpretation, he deviates to the first church to explain the concept “left to other people.” Little did he realize this was a more accurate interpretation of the great image? In the above reference, Joseph equated the Kingdom of God and the Kingdom of Heaven. Later he said that the Kingdom of God would go fourth that the Kingdom of Heaven should come.

Since Joseph Smith, all the leaders of the Church have followed the precedence of the Great Image foretelling secular kingdoms. Joseph nor those that followed knew the times and the seasons of Daniel. If you say the stone cut out of the mountain without hands is yet to be, you will receive prejudice against any other alternative. That is the fundamental reason for this site. If you can solve for the key of Daniel you can solve for the times and the seasons of revelations. Keep in mind that the Lord always has a physical interpretation as well as a hidden spiritual interpretation. All spiritual interpretations in history followed a path to fit a specific worldview of the author. The truth will not work to anyone’s accepted worldview. In fact, when you see the truth it will more often than not be sweet to the taste, but bitter when fully understood. That is the reason for hiding the allegory until all ugliness recedes into the past. If the Lord revealed the truth before its time, we would give up trying. If the Mormons new of what was to come they would not make an effort to take the gospel as revealed to Joseph Smith to the world. Since the gospel has reach the world, we can now look to the redemption of Zion as the stone cut out of the mountain without hands.

Thou, O king, sawest, and behold a great image. This great image, whose brightness was excellent, stood before thee; and the form thereof was terrible. This image’s head was of fine gold, his breast and his arms of silver, his belly and his thighs of brass, His legs of iron, his feet part of iron and part of clay. Thou sawest till that a stone was cut out without hands, which smote the image upon his feet that were of iron and clay, and brake them to pieces. Then was the iron, the clay, the brass, the silver, and the gold, broken to pieces together, and became like the chaff of the summer threshingfloors; and the wind carried them away, that no place was found for them: and the stone that smote the image became a great mountain, and filled the whole earth.

This is the dream; and we will tell the interpretation thereof before the king. Thou, O king, art a king of kings (this is Christ who is the king of kings): for the God of heaven hath given thee a kingdom, power, and strength, and glory. (who was given more glory than Christ) And wheresoever the children of men dwell, the beasts of the field and the fowls of the heaven hath he given into thine hand, and hath made thee ruler over them all. (all was given into the hand of Christ) Thou art this head of gold (Christ is the head of gold). And after thee shall arise another kingdom inferior to thee (this is degraded Christianity under the gentiles), and another third kingdom of brass (This is the reformation, which shall bear rule over all the earth--the protestants establish Briton and America which did rule the world). And the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron (this is the restored church): forasmuch as iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things (the church subdued “all things” as in restore “all things” yet to subde “the Law of Consecration”) see dictionary for subdue):and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise (this is the breaking of the Law of Consecration). And whereas thou sawest the feet and toes, part of potters’ clay, and part of iron, the kingdom shall be divided (from the law of heaven); but there shall be in it of the strength of the iron, forasmuch as thou sawest the iron mixed with miry clay. And as the toes of the feet (foundation of the church) were part of iron, and part of clay, so the kingdom shall be partly strong (testimony of Jesus), and partly broken (rejection of the higher law through tradition). And whereas thou sawest iron mixed with miry clay, they shall mingle themselves with the seed of men (members do mingle with the seed of men): but they shall not cleave one to another (as in the law of consecration), even as iron is not mixed with clay. And in the days of these kings (does not mean ten kings but rather the gold, silver, brass, and iron) shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed: and the kingdom shall not be left to other people (as the gospel was originally left to the gentiles), but it shall break in pieces (the Mormon hierarchy) and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever (as the kingdom of heaven which shall come after the gospel is preached throughout the earth). Forasmuch as thou sawest that the stone was cut out of the mountain (of the Lords house) without hands (naturally through those who understand), and that it brake in pieces the iron, the brass, the clay, the silver, and the gold (all churches not living the Laws of Heaven); the great God hath made known to the king what shall come to pass hereafter: and the dream is certain, and the interpretation thereof sure. (Daniel 2:31–45) Correct interpretation indicated in bold.

In is important to understand that “without hand” is like the Lord allowing his plant to grow without tilling the soil or rolling up his sleeves to labor in his vineyard. A new tree will grow naturally from a rod out of the stump left by hewing the original tree down. The restoration was a branch grafted into the roots of the stump in order to preserve the roots of the mother tree. This was the root of Jesse or Joseph Smith. The rod of Jesse will come as a rod out of the stem of the tree. It will have all of the natural genetic material of the original Church of Jesus Christ. One may feel inclined to debate the above, but restrain until you read all of the visions of Daniel and compare them with the vision of the great Image. It is here that prophecy will become bitter when you compare the seven half times of Daniel with revelation. You will then eat the little book and know what John the Revelator understood.
