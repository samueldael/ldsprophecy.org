The Allegory

Isaiah uses the allegory of the root and the rod of Jesse

In order to understand Isaiah you have to understand the allegory in terms of grafting branches into a vine or tree in order to first preserve the roots but also in hopes that in time a rod will come forth from the base stem to overtake a main stem that produces perfect fruit. Sometimes the fruit is corrupt and other times the tree has been hewn down leaving a stem or stump. You can save the tree or vine by first grafting into a root a branch from another tree. This will preserve the roots, but the branch that grows out of the root may bare fruit for a time, but eventually the branch does not. There is a certain amount of genetic material still in the branch, but nothing sufficient to bare fruit continually.

A wise husbandman knows that if you can just keep the roots alive, the stem or stump will produce a rod that comes out of the base of the tree. If the tree is still standing, you can cut it down and the rod grows even faster. You can even cut the branch of the root back and let the rod, having the natural genetic material of the original tree. Eventually it will give fruit equal to the first seedling.

This process can be done over and over with many vines and fruit barring trees. From experience I have not seen a rod come fourth from a fruit tree. This could be and because of modern grafter, the tree is not natural. Some times the graft one branch into the roots of another in order to control the growth or develop a different fruit. The Lord does not try to control the growth. He knows the outcome and chops down the tree leaving a stump. He preserves the roots by grafting a branch out of the roots.

Isaiah 11:1

And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a Branch shall grow out of his roots: 

Nature produces the rod out of the stem when the roots are strong and the tree has become corrupt. For a branch to grow out of the roots, the branch does not bare good fruit unless the Husbandman grafts and watches over the branch. When he leaves the branch becomes corrupted.

Even when the wind would take down a giant tree, I could clear the residue and leave a stump. In time a rod would come out of the base of the stump or stem and eventually consume the entire old stump. This is the plan of Christianity according to Daniel.

You have to experience this to understand. You have to understand that a root or a branch cannot produce the intended fruit unless it is a natural rod coming out of the stem or base of the trunk. The stem is the essence of the genetic future of any tree. Roots can become corrupt and braches may stop barring fruit, but the stem is eternal as long as there are living roots and a stump. Some trees die, however without a branch to receive energy from the sun. This is what drives the rod out.

Both the sun and the water in the earth can keep the stem alive as long as there is both a root and a branch. Bearing the best fruit to aged tree requires a natural shoot from the stem to appear without the hand of the gardener.

Now the tree is Christianity.
