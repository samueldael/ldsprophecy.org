The reason there is no mention of the priesthood is that it is written in the allegory as so much prophecy has been done before. Mormons, to my recollection, only assume the priesthood is present and never refer to the above. This is because I have never heard a prophecy interpreted correctly. Throughout scriptures, the Lord has used arm and presence to hide what He is saying about the Higher Priesthood. The Lord used many prophecies in allegory to Joseph Smith. Was this to keep certain things from the Saints until the time of the end? If so God has an alternate plan when his people refuse heaven. This was the case with Moses but He had the power of heaven but destroyed the tablets to keep it from Israel. In this case Joseph Smith did not understand, otherwise, we would have an interpretation. The code word in the above is the word presence.  Simply substitute the higher priesthood in place of the word presence and you will understand.

In the above, the Lord talks about a man like unto Moses. We are led to think this is Joseph Smith because the Lord is giving the Saints one last chance to Redeem Zion. When the Lord brought up the man the second time, he said, ÒI say unto you, that my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., is the man to whom I likened the servant to whom the Lord of the vineyard spake in the parable which I have given unto you.Ó

The Lord admits that He gave a parable. A parable to the Lord is an allegory for prophecy or a wise example. Those who understand that it is not Joseph Smith, although he had the keys to gather Israel, they should consider either the one mighty and strong or the rod of Jesse—both of these are mentioned in the Doctrine Covenants as relative to power. Power is the keyword to represent some power to bring the Redemption of Zion.
